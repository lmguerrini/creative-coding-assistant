# Demo Narrative

## One-sentence story

Creative Coding Assistant helps creative coders move from ambiguous audiovisual
intent to a bounded, previewable browser artifact while keeping sources,
workflow, runtime evidence, privacy, and limitations visible.

## SCR frame

**Situation.** Creative technologists work across fragmented documentation,
runtime APIs, visual systems, audio policies, and debugging tools.

**Complication.** A generic assistant can return plausible code without proving
that the right source was used, the selected workflow actually ran, the
artifact matches a supported runtime, or the preview succeeded.

**Resolution.** Creative Coding Assistant combines local retrieval, observable
Single/Multi/Auto orchestration, generated artifact extraction, bounded browser
previews, refinement and persistence, and evidence-separated evaluation in one
local workstation.

## The six-part showcase story

### 1. The problem

**Show:** the clean workstation and one expressive prompt.

**Say:**

> A creative coder may know the experience they want—recursive growth,
> orbital motion, a generative score—but not the exact runtime contract. The
> hard part is not producing code-shaped text. It is preserving intent while
> selecting realistic APIs, producing one inspectable artifact, running it
> safely, and explaining what was actually validated.

**Reviewer takeaway:** this is a creative translation and evidence problem, not
another generic chatbot.

### 2. The solution

**Show:** Demo Mode, then the Architecture and Workflow Dashboard pages.

**Say:**

> The product combines a Python workflow and local Chroma retrieval with a
> Next.js creative workstation. A request carries an explicit Single-Agent,
> Multi-Agent, or Auto selection. The runtime publishes the resolved route,
> artifacts, preview state, and recovery state instead of asking the UI to
> guess what happened.

**Reviewer takeaway:** the architecture is visible through the product, and the
workflow graph reflects execution evidence.

### 3. The data

**Show:** Knowledge Base, Retrieval, and the canonical retrieval report.

**Say:**

> The knowledge layer indexes registered technical material locally with
> embeddings and Chroma. In the dated canonical snapshot, 1,445 records support
> seven benchmark queries. Every query returned five chunks. Requested-domain
> coverage is 18 of 19; substantive expected-source overlap is 16 of 23. Those
> are retrieval-selection measures, not answer-quality percentages.

**Reviewer takeaway:** data lineage and missing coverage are explicit.

### 4. Evaluation

**Show:** Evaluation Lab, then the four canonical browser fixtures.

**Say:**

> Evaluation is split into Retrieval, AI/RAGAS, Creative, and
> Workflow/Reliability lanes. The approved four-row sanitized RAGAS fixture has
> a 61.44 percent macro across four measured dimensions, but it is historical
> and not comparable to current retrieval. Context Recall is missing. For
> runtime behavior, deterministic browser fixtures prove request identity,
> rendering, fullscreen, refinement, persistence, and reload. They do not prove
> a new model generation.

**Reviewer takeaway:** metrics are useful because their denominators and limits
are visible.

### 5. The hardest challenge

**Show:** Recursive aurora garden, enter/exit fullscreen, then submit its
palette refinement with an ordinary pointer click.

**Say:**

> The most instructive product bug appeared only after fullscreen restore at a
> compact viewport. Nested scroll and hover geometry could intercept a real
> refinement click. The fix changed stacking and motion, but the important
> decision was to keep the ordinary click in the regression. The four exact
> refinement flows then passed four of four, and the full Playwright suite
> passed twenty-eight of twenty-eight.

**Reviewer takeaway:** the project used evidence from realistic interaction,
not only unit contracts.

### 6. What is next

**Show:** Future Work and the multimodal scenario without sending private media.

**Say:**

> The highest-value next steps are evidence closure, not more feature labels:
> a current public-safe RAG evaluation with reference answers, a controlled
> image-influence study, final presentation-machine rehearsal, accessibility
> evidence, and decomposition of the largest modules. Image transport is
> already proven, but image influence is not, so I do not claim complete
> multimodal quality today.

**Reviewer takeaway:** the roadmap follows current gaps and does not inflate
partial capabilities.

## Flagship visual sequence

The catalog contains ten flows, but the live visual story uses four canonical
browser fixtures:

1. **Polyrhythmic constellation** — open silently; audio remains optional.
2. **Recursive aurora garden** — move the pointer to show direct interaction.
3. **Kinetic orbit sculpture** — use fullscreen and identify bundled Three.js
   r176, nested rigs, and changing frame evidence.
4. **Fractal solar bloom** — show the compact WebGL 1 shader boundary.

Do not call these four fixtures configured-provider outputs unless that exact
run has been captured separately. The truthful phrase is:

> These are deterministic browser-validation artifacts. They prove the product
> path and renderers; they do not stand in for a fresh model-quality result.

## SMART close

- **Specific:** demonstrate one end-to-end browser artifact and three supporting
  runtime surfaces.
- **Measurable:** cite 7/7 retrieval execution, 18/19 domain coverage, 16/23
  source anchors, four exact showcase smokes, and dated full-suite results.
- **Achievable:** use the tested local path and keep an offline artifact ready.
- **Relevant:** connect RAG, bounded agents, code generation, evaluation, and
  ethics to one creative-coding problem.
- **Time-bound:** finish the story in 10 minutes and reserve 5 minutes for
  evidence-focused questions.

## Fallback transitions

**Configured service unavailable:**

> The configured service is unavailable in this environment. I’m keeping the
> prompt visible and switching to a deterministic local artifact. This proves
> the workstation and renderer path, not a new provider response.

**Retrieval unavailable:**

> Current retrieval is unavailable, so I will show the dated machine-readable
> report and will not invent current citations.

**Preview unavailable:**

> The renderer is unavailable, so I am showing the source and its tested
> runtime boundary. I am not treating code presence as a successful preview.

**Audio unsuitable for the room:**

> The Tone.js fixture is intentionally silent-ready. I will keep it muted and
> demonstrate its visual and control state without claiming playback quality.
